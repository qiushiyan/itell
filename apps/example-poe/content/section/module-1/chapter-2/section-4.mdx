---
title: Key Terms
---

<Keyterm label="absolute advantage">
  the ability of an individual, firm, or country to carry out a particular
  economic activity or produce a particular good or service more efficiently
  than another individual, firm, or country.
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="allocative efficiency">
  when the mix of goods produced represents the mix that society most desires
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="budget constraint">
  all possible consumption combinations of goods that someone can afford, given
  the prices of goods, when all income is spent; the boundary of the opportunity
  set
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="comparative advantage">
  when a country can produce a good at a lower cost in terms of other goods; or,
  when a country has a lower opportunity cost of production
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="invisible hand">
Adam Smith's concept that individuals' self-interested behavior can lead to positive social outcomes

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="law of diminishing marginal utility">
  as we consume more of a good or service, the utility we get from additional
  units of the good or service tends to become smaller than what we received
  from earlier units
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="law of diminishing returns">
  as we add additional increments of resources to producing a good or service,
  the marginal benefit from those additional increments will decline
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="marginal analysis">
examination of decisions on the margin, meaning a little more or a little less from the status quo

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="normative statement">
  statement which describes how the world should be
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="opportunity cost">
  measures cost by what we give up/forfeit in exchange; opportunity cost
  measures the value of the forgone alternative
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="opportunity set">
  all possible combinations of consumption that someone can afford given the
  prices of goods and the individual's income
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="positive statement">
  statement which describes the world as it is
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="production possibilities frontier (PPF)">
  a diagram that shows the productively efficient combinations of two products
  that an economy can produce given the resources it has available.
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="productive efficiency">
  when it is impossible to produce more of one good (or service) without
  decreasing the quantity produced of another good (or service)
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="sunk costs">
  costs that we make in the past that we cannot recover
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="utility">
  satisfaction, usefulness, or value one obtains from consuming goods and
  services
</Keyterm>
