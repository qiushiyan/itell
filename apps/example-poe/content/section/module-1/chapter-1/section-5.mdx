---
title: "Key Terms"
---

<Keyterm label="circular flow diagram">
  a diagram that views the economy as consisting of households and firms
  interacting in a goods and services market and a labor market.
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="command economy">
  an economy where economic decisions are passed down from government authority
  and where the government owns the resources
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="division of labor">
  the way in which different workers divide required tasks to produce a good or
  service
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="economics">
  the study of how humans make choices under conditions of scarcity
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="economies of scale">
  when the average cost of producing each individual unit declines as total
  output increases
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="exports">
  products (goods and services) made domestically and sold abroad
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="fiscal policy">
  economic policies that involve government spending and taxes
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="globalization">
  the trend in which buying and selling in markets have increasingly crossed
  national borders
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="goods and services market">
  a market in which firms are sellers of what they produce and households are
  buyers
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="gross domestic product (GDP) ">
  measure of the size of total production in an economy
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="imports">
  products (goods and services) made abroad and then sold domestically
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="labor market">
  the market in which households sell their labor as workers to business firms
  or other employers
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="macroeconomics">
  the branch of economics that focuses on broad issues such as growth,
  unemployment, inflation, and trade balance
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="market">
  interaction between potential buyers and sellers; a combination of demand and
  supply
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="market economy">
  an economy where economic decisions are decentralized, private individuals own
  resources, and businesses supply goods and services based on demand
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="microeconomics">
  the branch of economics that focuses on actions of particular agents within
  the economy, like households, workers, and business firms
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="monetary policy">
  policy that involves altering the level of interest rates, the availability of
  credit in the economy, and the extent of borrowing
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="private enterprise">
  system where private individuals or groups of private individuals own and
  operate the means of production (resources and businesses)
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="scarcity">
  when human wants for goods and services exceed the available supply
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="specialization">
  when workers or firms focus on particular tasks for which they are well-suited
  within the overall production process
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="theory">
  a representation of an object or situation that is simplified while including
  enough of the key features to help us understand the object or situation
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="traditional economy">
  typically an agricultural economy where things are done the same as they have
  always been done
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="underground economy">
  typically an a market where the buyers and sellers make transactions in
  violation of one or more government regulations economy where things are done
  the same as they have always been done
</Keyterm>
