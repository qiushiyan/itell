<Keyterm label="anti-dumping laws">
  laws that block imports sold below the cost of production and impose tariffs
  that would increase the price of these imports to reflect their cost of
  production
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="common market">
  economic agreement between countries to allow free trade in goods, services,
  labor, and financial capital between members while having a common external
  trade policy
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="disruptive market change">
  innovative new product or production technology which disrupts the status quo
  in a market, leading the innovators to earn more income and profits and the
  other firms to lose income and profits, unless they can come up with their own
  innovations
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="dumping">
  selling internationally traded goods below their cost of production
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="economic union">
  economic agreement between countries to allow free trade between members, a
  common external trade policy, and coordinated monetary and fiscal policies
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="free trade agreement">
  economic agreement between countries to allow free trade between members
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT)">
  forum in which nations could come together to negotiate reductions in tariffs
  and other barriers to trade; the precursor to the World Trade Organization
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="Import quotas">
  numerical limits on the quantity of products that a country can import
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="national interest argument">
  the argument that there are compelling national interests against depending on
  key imports from other nations
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="nontariff barriers">
  ways a nation can draw up rules, regulations, inspections, and paperwork to
  make it more costly or difficult to import products
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="protectionism">
  government policies to reduce or block imports
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="race to the bottom">
  when production locates in countries with the lowest environmental (or other)
  standards, putting pressure on all countries to reduce their environmental
  standards
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="World Trade Organization (WTO)">
  organization that seeks to negotiate reductions in barriers to trade and to
  adjudicate complaints about violations of international trade policy;
  successor to the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT)
</Keyterm>
