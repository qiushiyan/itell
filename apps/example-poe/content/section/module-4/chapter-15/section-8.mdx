<Keyterm label="automatic stabilizers">
  tax and spending rules that have the effect of slowing down the rate of
  decrease in aggregate demand when the economy slows down and restraining
  aggregate demand when the economy speeds up, without any additional change in
  legislation
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="balanced budget">
  when government spending and taxes are equal
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="budget deficit">
  when the federal government spends more money than it receives in taxes in a
  given year
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="budget surplus">
  when the government receives more money in taxes than it spends in a year
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="contractionary fiscal policy">
  fiscal policy that decreases the level of aggregate demand, either through
  cuts in government spending or increases in taxes
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="corporate income tax">
  a tax imposed on corporate profits
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="crowding out">
  federal spending and borrowing causes interest rates to rise and business
  investment to fall
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="discretionary fiscal policy">
  the government passes a new law that explicitly changes overall tax or
  spending levels with the intent of influencing the level of overall economic
  activity
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="estate and gift tax">
  a tax on people who pass assets to the next generation—either after death or
  during life in the form of gifts
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="excise tax">
  a tax on a specific good—on gasoline, tobacco, and alcohol
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="expansionary fiscal policy">
  fiscal policy that increases the level of aggregate demand, either through
  increases in government spending or cuts in taxes
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="implementation lag">
  the time it takes for the funds relating to fiscal policy to be dispersed to
  the appropriate agencies to implement the programs
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="individual income tax">
  a tax based on the income, of all forms, received by individuals
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="legislative lag">
  the time it takes to get a fiscal policy bill passed
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="marginal tax rates">
  or the tax that must be paid on all yearly income
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="national debt">
  the total accumulated amount the government has borrowed, over time, and not
  yet paid back
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="payroll tax">
  a tax based on the pay received from employers; the taxes provide funds for
  Social Security and Medicare
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="progressive tax">
  a tax that collects a greater share of income from those with high incomes
  than from those with lower incomes
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="proportional tax">
  a tax that is a flat percentage of income earned, regardless of level of
  income
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="recognition lag">
  the time it takes to determine that a recession has occurred
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="regressive tax">
  a tax in which people with higher incomes pay a smaller share of their income
  in tax
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="standardized employment budget">
  the budget deficit or surplus in any given year adjusted for what it would
  have been if the economy were producing at potential GDP
</Keyterm>
