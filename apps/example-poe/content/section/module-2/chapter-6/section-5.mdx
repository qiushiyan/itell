<Keyterm label="aggregate production function">
  the process whereby an economy as a whole turns economic inputs such as human
  capital, physical capital, and technology into output measured as GDP per
  capita
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="capital deepening">
  an increase by society in the average level of physical and/or human capital
  per person
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="compound growth rate">
  the rate of growth when multiplied by a base that includes past GDP growth
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="contractual rights">
  the rights of individuals to enter into agreements with others regarding the
  use of their property providing recourse through the legal system in the event
  of noncompliance
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="convergence">
  pattern in which economies with low per capita incomes grow faster than
  economies with high per capita incomes
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="human capital">
  the accumulated skills and education of workers
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="Industrial Revolution">
  the widespread use of power-driven machinery and the economic and social
  changes that occurred in the first half of the 1800s
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="infrastructure">
  a component of physical capital such as roads and rail systems
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="innovation">
  putting advances in knowledge to use in a new product or service
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="invention">advances in knowledge</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="labor productivity">
  the value of what is produced per worker, or per hour worked (sometimes called
  worker productivity)
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="modern economic growth">
  the period of rapid economic growth from 1870 onward
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="physical capital">
  the plant and equipment that firms use in production; this includes
  infrastructure
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="production function">
  the process whereby a firm turns economic inputs like labor, machinery, and
  raw materials into outputs like goods and services that consumers use
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="rule of law">
  the process of enacting laws that protect individual and entity rights to use
  their property as they see fit. Laws must be clear, public, fair, and
  enforced, and applicable to all members of society
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="special economic zone (SEZ)">
  area of a country, usually with access to a port where, among other benefits,
  the government does not tax trade
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="technological change">
  a combination of invention—advances in knowledge—and innovation
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="technology">
  all the ways in which existing inputs produce more or higher quality, as well
  as different and altogether new products
</Keyterm>
