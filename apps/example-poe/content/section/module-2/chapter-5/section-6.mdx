---
title: "Key Terms"
---

<Keyterm label="business cycle">

the economy's relatively short-term movement in and out of recession

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="depreciation">

the process by which capital ages over time and therefore loses its value

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="depression">

an especially lengthy and deep decline in output

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="double counting">

a potential mistake to avoid in measuring GDP, in which output is counted more
than once as it travels through the stages of production

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="durable good">
  long-lasting good like a car or a refrigerator
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="exchange rate">
  the price of one currency in terms of another currency
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="final good and service">
  output used directly for consumption, investment, government, and trade
  purposes; contrast with “intermediate good”
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="GDP per capita">GDP divided by the population</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="gross domestic product (GDP)">
  the value of the output of all final goods and services produced within a
  country in a year
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="gross national product (GNP)">
  includes what is produced domestically and what is produced by domestic labor
  and business abroad in a year
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="intermediate good">
  output provided to other businesses at an intermediate stage of production,
  not for final users; contrast with “final good and service”
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="inventory">
  good that has been produced, but not yet been sold
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="national income">
  includes all income earned: wages, profits, rent, and profit income
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="net national product (NNP)">

GNP minus depreciation

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="nominal value">
  the economic statistic actually announced at that time, not adjusted for
  inflation; contrast with real value
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="nondurable good">
  short-lived good like food and clothing
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="peak">
  during the business cycle, the highest point of output before a recession
  begins
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="real value">
  an economic statistic after it has been adjusted for inflation; contrast with
  nominal value
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="recession">

a significant decline in national output

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="service">

product which is intangible (in contrast to goods) such as entertainment, healthcare, or education

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="standard of living">

all elements that affect people's happiness, whether people buy or sell these elements in the market or not

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="structure">

building used as residence, factory, office building, retail store, or for other purposes

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="trade balance">

gap between exports and imports

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="trade deficit">

exists when a nation's imports exceed its exports and it calculates them as
$\text{imports} - \text{exports}$

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="trade surplus">

exists when a nation's exports exceed its imports and it calculates them as
$\text{exports} - \text{imports}$

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="trough">

during the business cycle, the lowest point of output in a recession, before a recovery begins

</Keyterm>
