---
title: Key Terms
---

<Keyterm label="adverse selection of wage cuts argument">
  if employers reduce wages for all workers, the best will leave
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="cyclical unemployment">
  unemployment closely tied to the business cycle, like higher unemployment
  during a recession
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label ="discouraged workers">
 those who have stopped looking for employment due to the lack of suitable positions available

</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="efficiency wage theory">
  the theory that the productivity of workers, either individually or as a
  group, will increase if the employer pays them more
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="frictional unemployment">
  unemployment that occurs as workers move between jobs
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="implicit contract">
  an unwritten agreement in the labor market that the employer will try to keep
  wages from falling when the economy is weak or the business is having trouble,
  and the employee will not expect huge salary increases when the economy or the
  business is strong
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="insider-outsider model">
  those already working for the firm are “insiders” who know the procedures; the
  other workers are “outsiders” who are recent or prospective hires
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="labor force participation rate">
  this is the percentage of adults in an economy who are either employed or who
  are unemployed and looking for a job
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="natural rate of unemployment">
  the unemployment rate that would exist in a growing and healthy economy from
  the combination of economic, social, and political factors that exist at a
  given time
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="out of the labor force">
  those who are not working and not looking for work—whether they want
  employment or not; also termed “not in the labor force”
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="relative wage coordination argument">
  across-the-board wage cuts are hard for an economy to implement, and workers
  fight against them
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="structural unemployment">
  unemployment that occurs because individuals lack skills valued by employers
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="underemployed">
  individuals who are employed in a job that is below their skills
</Keyterm>

<Keyterm label="unemployment rate">
  the percentage of adults who are in the labor force and thus seeking jobs, but
  who do not have jobs
</Keyterm>
